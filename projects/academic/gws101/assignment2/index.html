Patriarchal Dominance as a Structural Issue

An image depicting a gender pyramid by pelicanweb.org

Patriarchal culture is deeply embedded in almost every area of human existence. We as a society participate in this patriarchy, whether we like to or not, because the patriarchy is a structural issue.

At its core, patriarchy is... about humanity, with manhood and masculinity most closely associated with being human and womanhood and femininity related to the marginal position of "other."
Allan G. Johnson. Patriarchy, the System.
Social relationships and the normativity of men being the dominant power contribute highly to the structure we have now. "Structure" in this sense, is defined by ways that gender privilege and oppression contribute to unequal rights and fairness for others. Because manhood being the dominant power is the normal, we create an uequal fighting ground where anybody other than what's viewed as normal will always lose.

Intersectionality and Systems of Power

An image showing intersectionality by AAPF.org

As discussed earlier, patriarchy favors those who are the dominant power. However, the reality is that most people are and will be disadvantaged because of one factor or another.

Intersectionality was initially conceived as a way to present a simple reality... that disadvantage or exclusion can be based on the interaction of multiple factors rather than just one.
African American Policy Forum. “A Primer on Intersectionality.”
The law impacts people differently based on their differing experiences and identities. The systems of power dictate that those who possess qualities that are lower on the social hierarchy will be treated unfairly. Intersectionality hopes to highlight that different combinations of people will face different issues than others, and that we as a society must pay attention to the those intersections.

Masculinity as a Social Construct

Masculinity is a social construct. Many have defined masculinity as the way in which men act in accordance to the normative patriarchy. However, this definition weakens society and fails to highlight those who don't fall into this definition. Therefore, what arrived was a model of "multiple masculinities."

Men enact and embody different configurations of masculinity depending on their positions within a social hierarchy of power.
Pascoe. Dude, You're a Fag: Masculinity and Sexuality in High School.
This model shows that men too fall under the guise of Intersectionality, where depending where on the hierarchy they fall they have wildly different forms of masculinity.

White Privilege and Male Privilege

We live in a white patriarchy. That means that those with power have the ability to control others, even if unconsciously. More specifically, this manifests itself in white and male privilege. Furthermore, those in power are carefully thought not to pay attention to their privilege. Privilege comes in many ways.

I have come to see white privilege as an invisible package of unearned assets that I can count on cashing in each day, but about which I was "meant" to remain oblivious. White privilege is like an invisible weightless knapsack of special provisions, assurances, tools, maps, guides, codebooks, passports, visas, clothes, compass, emergency gear, and blank checks.
McIntosh, Peggy. White Privilege and Male Privilege.
Privilege presents itself in all facets of life, and it is because of this that many who benefit from it believe it to be the right way to live. However, those on the other side lament the fact that they must work exponentially harder just to be at the same starting line.

Seeing One's Self

An image showing the cycle of socialization

The ability to see one's self in the systems of power and being able to understand why you do or don't have privilege is an invaluable lesson to be learned. You cannot choose how you are born, you only just are. Because of this, you are able to choose whether or not to try and change the patriarchy, as shown in the cycle of socialization above. It does not matter where you were placed in the patriarchy. If you struggle at all, then you can connect with others and strive for change.

There is a special tie binding people together who struggle collectively for liberation.
bell hooks. MEN: Comrades in Struggle.
